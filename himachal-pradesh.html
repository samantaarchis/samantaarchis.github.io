Tourist Destinations

  • Shimla: The former summer capital of British India, known for its colonial architecture and scenic views.
  • Manali: A popular hill station with adventure activities like paragliding and skiing.
  • Dharamshala: Home to the Dalai Lama and Tibetan monasteries.
  • Rohtang Pass: A high-altitude pass offering breathtaking views and adventure sports.
  • Kullu: Known for its apple orchards and the Kullu Dussehra festival.
  • Chamba: Famous for its temples and the Minjar festival.

Nature Tourism

Wildlife

Great Himalayan National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to snow leopards, Himalayan black bears, western tragopans, and monals.

Hills and Valleys

Shimla, Manali, Dharamshala, Kasauli, and Kinnaur offer stunning mountain views and valleys.

Lakes and Fields

Renuka Lake, a sacred site, and various alpine meadows.

Religious and Pilgrimage Sites

  • Jwalamukhi Temple: A Shakti Peeth where flames emerge from the rocks.
  • Chintpurni: Another Shakti Peeth with a temple dedicated to Goddess Chhinnamastika.
  • Naina Devi Temple: A temple with a sacred spring.
  • Bajreshwari Mata Temple: Located in Kangra, a major pilgrimage site.
  • Shri Chamunda Devi Mandir: A temple in Kangra district.
  • Hidimba Devi Temple: An ancient temple in Manali.
