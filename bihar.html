Bihar Tourism

Explore the ancient heritage, Buddhist sites, and natural beauty of Bihar

Tourist Destinations

  • Rajgir: Ancient capital of Magadha, known for hot springs, Rajgir Glass Sky Walk Bridge, and Rajgir Zoo Safari.
  • Nalanda: Site of the ancient Nalanda University, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with ruins of monasteries and temples.
  • Bodh Gaya: Birthplace of Buddhism, home to the Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Patna: Capital city with the Khuda Bakhsh Library, Patna Museum, and Golghar.
  • Gaya: Known for the Vishnupad Temple and Bodh Gaya nearby.
  • Munger: Fort, Kastaharni Ghat, and Sita Kund.
  • Darbhanga: Darbhanga Fort and historical sites.

Nature Tourism

  • Valmiki National Park: Tiger reserve with diverse wildlife, including Bengal tigers and Gangetic dolphins.
  • Vikramshila Gangetic Dolphin Sanctuary: Home to the endangered Gangetic dolphin.
  • Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary: Largest oxbow lake in Asia, Ramsar site with migratory birds.
  • Bhimbandh Wildlife Sanctuary: Forests and wildlife in the Kaimur hills.
  • Gautam Buddha Wildlife Sanctuary: Protected area with diverse flora and fauna.
  • Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary: Hills and forests with wildlife.
  • Udaypur Wildlife Sanctuary: Wetlands and grasslands.

Religious Sites

  • Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya): UNESCO World Heritage Site where Buddha attained enlightenment.
  • Nalanda Mahavihara: Ancient Buddhist university ruins.
  • Rajgir: Buddhist and Jain pilgrimage sites, including Gridhakuta Hill.
  • Vishnupad Temple (Gaya): Hindu temple for ancestor worship.
  • Sitamarhi: Birthplace of Goddess Sita.
  • Vasupujya Temple: Jain temple for the 12th Tirthankara.
  • Barabar Caves: Ancient Buddhist caves.
