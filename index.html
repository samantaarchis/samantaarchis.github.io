States of India

    States of India

    • Andhra Pradesh

      Known for its rich cultural heritage, temples, and coastal beauty. Capital: Amaravati.

    • Arunachal Pradesh

      The land of the rising sun, famous for its biodiversity and tribal cultures. Capital: Itanagar.

    • Assam

      Home to the Brahmaputra River, tea gardens, and wildlife sanctuaries. Capital: Dispur.

    • Bihar

      Birthplace of Buddhism and Jainism, with historical sites like Bodh Gaya. Capital: Patna.

    • Chhattisgarh

      Known for its forests, waterfalls, and tribal culture. Capital: Raipur.

    • Goa

      A popular tourist destination with beautiful beaches and Portuguese influence. Capital: Panaji.

    • Gujarat

      Famous for its festivals, wildlife, and industrial growth. Capital: Gandhinagar.

    • Haryana

      Known for its agricultural productivity and historical forts. Capital: Chandigarh.

    • Himachal Pradesh

      The Devbhumi, famous for its hill stations and adventure sports. Capital: Shimla.

    • Jharkhand

      Rich in minerals and waterfalls, with a strong tribal heritage. Capital: Ranchi.

    • Karnataka

      Home to Bengaluru, coffee plantations, and ancient temples. Capital: Bengaluru.

    • Kerala

      God's Own Country, known for backwaters, spices, and Ayurveda. Capital: Thiruvananthapuram.

    • Madhya Pradesh

      The heart of India, with ancient temples and wildlife reserves. Capital: Bhopal.

    • Maharashtra

      Home to Mumbai, forts, and diverse cultures. Capital: Mumbai.

    • Manipur

      Known for its dance forms, lakes, and martial arts. Capital: Imphal.

    • Meghalaya

      The abode of clouds, famous for living root bridges and rainfall. Capital: Shillong.

    • Mizoram

      The land of the hill people, with bamboo forests and festivals. Capital: Aizawl.

    • Nagaland

      Known for its tribes, festivals, and natural beauty. Capital: Kohima.

    • Odisha

      Famous for its temples, beaches, and tribal art. Capital: Bhubaneswar.

    • Punjab

      The land of five rivers, known for its cuisine and history. Capital: Chandigarh.

    • Rajasthan

      The royal state, with palaces, deserts, and vibrant culture. Capital: Jaipur.

    • Sikkim

      The hidden paradise, known for monasteries and trekking. Capital: Gangtok.

    • Tamil Nadu

      Rich in Dravidian culture, temples, and classical music. Capital: Chennai.

    • Telangana

      Known for its IT hub Hyderabad and ancient history. Capital: Hyderabad.

    • Tripura

      Famous for its palaces, lakes, and tribal heritage. Capital: Agartala.

    • Uttar Pradesh

      The most populous state, with the Taj Mahal and Ganges River. Capital: Lucknow.

    • Uttarakhand

      The land of gods, known for pilgrimage sites and Himalayas. Capital: Dehradun.

    • West Bengal

      Home to Kolkata, Sunderbans, and literary heritage. Capital: Kolkata.

Union Territories of India

  • Andaman and Nicobar Islands

    A group of islands in the Bay of Bengal, known for beaches and biodiversity.

  • Chandigarh

    A planned city serving as the capital of Punjab and Haryana, known for its architecture.

  • Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu

    Former Portuguese enclaves, known for their beaches and historical forts.

  • Delhi

    The national capital, a hub of history, culture, and politics.

  • Jammu and Kashmir

    Known for its natural beauty, Kashmir Valley, and diverse cultures.

  • Ladakh

    A cold desert region, famous for monasteries and high-altitude lakes.

  • Lakshadweep

    A group of coral islands, known for marine life and lagoons.

  • Puducherry

    A former French colony, known for its colonial architecture and beaches.
