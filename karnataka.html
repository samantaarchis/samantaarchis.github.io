Tourist Destinations

  • Hampi: Ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple, and rocky landscapes.
  • Pattadakal: UNESCO World Heritage Site with 8th-century temples in Dravidian and Nagara styles.
  • Badami: Cave temples carved into sandstone hills, capital of the Chalukya dynasty.
  • Aihole: Known as the cradle of Indian architecture, over 140 temples from various dynasties.
  • Bijapur: Gol Gumbaz, the world's second-largest dome, and other monuments from the Adil Shahi dynasty.
  • Gokarna: Coastal town with Mahabaleshwara Temple and Om Beach.
  • Udupi: Krishna Temple, Paryaya festival, and Malpe Beach.
  • Mysore: Mysore Palace, cultural capital with palaces, gardens, and museums.
  • Belur: Chennakeshava Temple, a masterpiece of Hoysala architecture.
  • Halebidu: Hoysaleswara Temple, intricate carvings and sculptures.
  • Shravanabelagola: Massive monolithic statue of Gomateshwara (Bahubali).
  • Somanathapura: Kesava Temple, another Hoysala architectural marvel.

Nature Tourism

Beaches

Gokarna, Murdeshwar, Karwar, Malpe, and St. Mary's Island offer pristine coastal beauty.

Hill Stations

Kemmangundi, Kudremukh, Agumbe, Kodachadri, Biligiriranga Hills, and Baba Budangiri provide scenic views and trekking opportunities.

National Parks and Wildlife

Bandipur National Park, Nagarahole National Park, Kudremukh National Park, Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary, and Anshi National Park are home to tigers, elephants, and diverse flora.

Waterfalls

Jog Falls (highest in India), Gokak Falls, Magod Falls, Lushington Falls, and Shivanasamudram Falls.

Caves

Badami cave temples, Yana caves, and other natural formations.

Religious and Pilgrimage Sites

  • Virupaksha Temple (Hampi): Dedicated to Shiva, a major pilgrimage site.
  • Mahabaleshwara Temple (Gokarna): One of the twelve Jyotirlingas.
  • Krishna Temple (Udupi): Center of Dvaita philosophy, founded by Madhwa.
  • Chennakeshava Temple (Belur): Hoysala temple with exquisite carvings.
  • Hoysaleswara Temple (Halebidu): Twin Shiva temples showcasing Hoysala art.
  • Kesava Temple (Somanathapura): Star-shaped temple with detailed sculptures.
  • Gomateshwara (Shravanabelagola): Jain pilgrimage site with the tallest monolithic statue.
  • Mookambika Temple (Kollur): Goddess temple on Kodachadri hill.
  • Gol Gumbaz (Bijapur): Mausoleum with unique acoustics.
  • Jain Basadis: Numerous Jain temples like those in Karkala and Mudabidri.
